Intro

Neptune is generally the eight planet from the Sun, an of the four gas giants, it is the fourth and final. It is similar to Uranus, although Uranus is larger than it. It has a number of rings made up of various particles, as with the other gas giants.

Johann Galle, a German, found it.

Composition and Atmosphere

Neptune’s core is small and rocky, covered by water, ammonia and methane. It’s atmosphere is mainly made up of three gases: Hydrogen, helium, and methane. Methane is the reason for its blue colour.

It has a large spot (known as the “Great Dark Spot”), which is as big as the earth. It goes in the opposite direction to that of Neptune’s rotation, reaching speeds of 1000 kilometres per hour. This, however, is only half the speed of the winds on Neptune. Another such anticyclone, the “Small Dark Spot”, is present in the atmosphere of Neptune.

At a less windy altitude, the “Scooter” remains at the same relative position. It moves with the direction of the core, hence going in the opposite direction tio the Great and Small Dark Spots.

Ring System

Neptune has a ring system , which covers an area between 40,000 and 63,000 km from the planet. It has three rings. The Adams ring, named after an astronomer who predicted its existence, as did the astronomer who had the Le Verrier ring named after him. Astronomers have yet to explain the existence of clumps of ring material in the Adams ring, which was found by the Voyager II

Moons

Neptune has eight moons. Two of them  were known to exist prior to Voyager II’s investigation of Neptune. These two moons were Triton and Nereid. Triton is covered in nitrogen, and has the lowest temperature of all known objects in our solar system. It is moving towards Neptune, and in under 100,000,000 years it enter into Roche limit of Neptune*. After this it could shatter into a new ring.

(The Roche limit is the closest distance one object can have to another before being destroyed by its gravitational forces).
