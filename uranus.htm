Intro

Uranus, discovered by William Herschel, is planet number seven in our solar system. It is the third largest, and. Uranus’ most defining feature is that, in regard to the rest of the planets, it is lying down, that is, it’s rotation angle is tilted by 98 degrees to earths plane of orbit.

Composition and Atmosphere

The core of Uranus is made of rock, and is visually featureless. The atmosphere is made up of hydrogen, helium, methane, and ammonia.

Due to the horizontal nature of Uranus, each pole spends 42 years in darkness, while the other pole would be enjoying 42 years of sunlight. This makes little difference, however,  to the temperatures of the poles, which would only vary by 2 degrees Celsius.

Ring System

Eleven rings are present around  Uranus. They are, however, invisible even through a telescope. They were first seen when they came between the earth and a star, causing that star’s light to hidden. In ’86 Voyager II took a closer and more detailed look at these rings.

Moons

15 moons have been found in orbit of Uranus. These moons are on the same plane as the tilted equator. This causes the orbit of the moons to be seen as circles at some times, and these circular orbits appear to get thinner until the orbit of Uranus’ moons appear as straight lines.

Of the fifteen moons, only five were proven to exist prior to Voyager II investigation of the planet. These five were Titania, the largest, Oberon, Ariel and Umbriel and Miranda
