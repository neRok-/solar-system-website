Intro

Mars is the most similar planet ot earth , despite the size difference (mars is about half the size of earth). It is similar in that its day is just 38 minutes longer than ours, the tilt of Mars is 1.7 degrees greater than Earth's, there are many of earths geological features present there (including clouds, volcanoes, canyons, mountains, deserts, and ice caps on each pole that change is size with the seasons). However, unlike Earth, mars is arid and lifeless. Dust collected from mars shows high iron content, which causes the redness of the planet.

Composition

Mars’ core is made of iron and rock. This core is has a diameter of about 2500 kilometres. A mantle encasing the solid core is made up silicate rock, and is around 2,000 kilometres thick. The crust is made of solid rock and water ice and is 45 kilometres thick.

Surface Features

Mars’ northern hemisphere has very few craters, due to the volcanoes that have continually been forming it over “recent” years, however the southern hemisphere has very many craters that are 3.5 billion years old.

Mars has two noticeable features: The largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons; and a canyon 7 kilometres deep and 600 kilometres wide; Valles Marineris, one of many canyons on mars, which may have been formed from water erosion.

Atmosphere

Mars’ atmosphere is mostly made up of carbon dioxide, with small amounts of nitrogen, argon, oxygen, carbon monoxide and water.

Moons

Mars has two moons, named Phobos and Deimos, meaning fear and panic. They are both much smaller than Earth’s moon. Phobos’ average diameter is 22 kilometres and Deimos’ is 13 kilometres. They could be asteroids pulled into Mars’ orbit, as they also are heavily cratered.
