The Milky-way Galaxy

Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is a spiral galaxy made up of stars, gas, and dust. When you look up into the night sky, every star you see belongs to our galaxy. Our Solar System exists in one of the arms of stars that spiral out from the centre of the galaxy, called the Orion arm. The Sun in our Solar System is just one of the 100 billion stars that are constantly travelling around the centre of the galaxy.

The Milky Way, contains a total of about 500 billion stars. It formed from a huge cloud of gas and dust about 10 billion years ago. At the centre is a dense, spherical nucleus of stars that may also contain a black hole. A disk of spiral arms containing hot young stars encircles the nucleus. The nucleus and disk are surrounded by a sparsely populated halo of very old stars.

The galaxy’s nucleus is about 10,000 light years in diameter. The disk is about 100,000 light years in diameter and 1,000 light years thick. Lastly the halo is about 50,000 light years in diameter. The Solar System (including the Öpik-Oort Cloud, a huge region of space populated only by comets) seems relatively small at only 3 light years across. The Sun takes about 250 million years to orbit the centre of the Galaxy at a speed of about 220 km per second. The Sun has made between 15 and 20 orbits so far.
